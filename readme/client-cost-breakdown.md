# client-cost-breakdown.md
# Client-Facing Cost Breakdown Templates
# Use these when presenting proposals to business owners — builds trust, reduces objections

---

## WHY SHOW CLIENTS A COST BREAKDOWN?

1. **Builds trust** — clients see you're not hiding anything
2. **Justifies your retainer** — they understand tools cost money to run
3. **Separates your fee from tool fees** — no confusion about where money goes
4. **Positions you as an expert** — you know exactly what's needed and why
5. **Reduces churn** — clients who understand the system value it more

---

## HOW TO USE THESE TEMPLATES

- Fill in the numbers from tool-pricing-guide.md for the specific client
- Present during proposal call or send as part of your proposal document
- Always show 3 columns: Tool Cost, Your Service Fee, Total Investment
- Frame tool costs as "infrastructure" — like rent for a physical shop

## 🗣️ HOW TO PRESENT THIS TO CLIENTS

### Script for Proposal Call:

> "I want to be completely transparent about how this is priced. Your monthly investment has two parts.
>
> First, there's the infrastructure — these are the actual tools that power your system. Think of it like the rent and electricity for a physical office. These are ₹[X]/month and you'll see exactly what each tool does.
>
> Second, there's our service fee — this covers our work to manage, maintain, and improve the system every month so you never have to think about it.
>
> Combined, your total monthly investment is ₹[X]. And based on what I've seen with similar businesses, you'll recover this within [X] months just from [specific benefit]. From month [X+1] onwards, it's pure profit."

---

## ⚡ OBJECTION HANDLING

**"Why is the monthly fee so high?"**
> "Most of that is infrastructure — tools that actively work for your business 24/7. Our service fee is actually only ₹[X]/month. That's less than hiring one part-time staff member who works 3 hours/week."

**"Can we do it cheaper?"**
> "Yes — we can start with free tiers. But at your size ([X members/clients]), you'll outgrow free within [X] months and have to upgrade anyway. Better to budget correctly from day one."

**"What if I want to cancel?"**
> "You own all your data. We'll export everything from Airtable for you. The tools run on your accounts — we just manage them. You can continue independently or we can hand it over to another developer."
